---
title: "Quickstart"
description: "Start exploring Argentine economic data in under a minute — no account or installation required."
---

Argentista is a web application — there's nothing to install. Open it in your browser and you're ready to go.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Argentista">
    Navigate to the Argentista web app in your browser.

    The home page loads two tools immediately:
    - **Inflation Calculator** — on the left
    - **Currency Converter** — on the right

    <Info>
      All data is fetched in real time from public APIs. The first load may take a moment while data is retrieved.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the Inflation Calculator">
    The Inflation Calculator shows you how the purchasing power of an amount of pesos has changed over time.

    1. Enter an **initial amount** in Argentine pesos (ARS) in the "Monto Inicial" field.
    2. Select the **start month and year** — the period from which you want to measure inflation.
    3. The **end month and year** default to the latest available data. Adjust if needed.
    4. Results update automatically:
       - **Monto Final** — the inflation-adjusted equivalent amount
       - **Total** — total inflation percentage over the period
       - **Promedio** — average monthly inflation rate
       - **Anual** — annualized inflation rate

    <Note>
      Data comes from INDEC (Argentina's official statistics bureau) via ArgentinaDatos. Coverage begins in January 2017.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore the Inflation page">
    Click **Inflación** in the navigation to go to `/inflacion`.

    This page shows two views of inflation data:

    - **Inflation Graph** (left) — an interactive line chart showing monthly inflation trends over time
    - **Inflation Indices** (right) — a collapsible table organized by decade and year

    In the indices table, click a decade to expand it, then click a year to see the monthly breakdown. Each year shows:
    - Minimum monthly rate (green)
    - Maximum monthly rate (red)
    - Average monthly rate (blue)

    <Tip>
      The most recent decade and year are expanded by default, so you can immediately see current-year data.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check dollar exchange rates">
    Click **Dólar** in the navigation to go to `/dolar`.

    The **Dollar Prices** section shows live buy (compra) and sell (venta) prices for all major Argentine dollar market types:

    | Type | Description |
    |------|-------------|
    | Oficial | Official government rate (BNA) |
    | Blue | Informal parallel market rate |
    | Bolsa (MEP) | Bond-implied electronic payment market |
    | Contado con Liqui | Capital flow settlement rate |
    | Mayorista | Wholesale rate for large transactions |
    | Cripto | Cryptocurrency-implied rate |
    | Tarjeta | Credit card rate (includes PAIS tax) |

    Each card shows a green pulsing indicator with the last update time.

    <Warning>
      The **blue dollar** is the informal/parallel market rate. It is not set by the government and trades at a premium to the official rate.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="View dollar price history">
    Scroll down on the `/dolar` page to the **Historical Dollar Chart**.

    Use the **Rango** dropdown to select a time range:
    - 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, 1 year, or all available history

    The chart displays price lines for all dollar types simultaneously, color-coded:
    - Sky blue — Oficial
    - Red — Blue
    - Orange — Bolsa
    - Purple — Contado con Liqui
    - Blue — Mayorista
    - Yellow — Cripto
    - Green — Tarjeta

    Hover over any point on the chart to see all prices for that date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Convert currency">
    Return to the home page (`/`) and use the **Currency Converter**.

    1. Select a **dollar type** from the dropdown (e.g., "Dólar Blue - $1200").
    2. Enter an amount in the input field.
    3. The result updates instantly.
    4. Click the **swap button** (↕) to reverse the conversion direction between ARS and USD.

    <Tip>
      The converter uses the **sell price (venta)** of your selected dollar type for all conversions.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
